The plucky Amore Opera often seems to fill gaps in the city’s season, and so it is with this production of Puccini’s classic love story. Scott Jackson Wiley conducts a production by Iris Karlin and Nathan Hull with a rotating cast.866-811-4111, amoreopera.orgMAKE MUSIC WINTER at various locations (Dec. 21). Make Music’s participatory programs on the winter solstice are dotted throughout the city, and take the form of roving performances. Full details are available online, but note a procession of kalimbas through Central Park at dusk, a hand bell parade in the East Village in the early evening, and a mix of Baroque flute, pantomime and singalong in Sara Delano Roosevelt Park at noon.makemusicny.orgNEW YORK STRING ORCHESTRA at Carnegie Hall (Dec. 24, 7 p.m.). The holiday season always means the annual performances from the young players of this orchestra, under the baton of Jaime Laredo. In their traditional Christmas Eve event, they play Mendelssohn’s “The Hebrides” Overture, Vivaldi’s Concerto for Four Violins in B minor and Beethoven’s Piano Concerto No. 4. Yefim Bronfman is the soloist.212-247-7800, carnegiehall.org‘EL NIÑO’ at the Met Cloisters (Dec. 21, 5:30 p.m.; Dec. 22, 1:30 and 5:30 p.m.). Julia Bullock’s residency at the Metropolitan Museum of Art has already garnered resounding praise for its consummate artistry and its social conscience, and its high standards ought to continue in these performances of John Adams’s Nativity oratorio. Given in a chamber version arranged by Preben Antonsen, it is performed by the members of the American Modern Opera Company, a loose collective of like-minded collaborators, as well as other guests. Alongside Bullock, the singers are J’Nai Bridges, Anthony Roth Costanzo and Davóne Tines; Christian Reif conducts. 212-535-7710, metmuseum.orgSONNAMBULA at the Met Cloisters (Dec. 23, 1 and 3 p.m.). This Renaissance ensemble is also in residence at the Met this year, in a position typically held by a string quartet. Here they are joined by a guest lutenist, Esteban La Rotta, for music from Spain in the late 15th and early 16th centuries.212-535-7710, metmuseum.orgAdvertisement
